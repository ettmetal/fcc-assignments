Ada Lovelace The first computer programmer

Background

Ada Lovelace was born in 1815. The daughter of Lord Byron and Lady Annabella Byron, their only legitemate child. Her mother insisted that she was tutored in subjects such as mathematics and music from an early age. This type of education was unusual for women at the time, yet Lady Byron herself was versed in mathematics, Lord Byron referring to her as his 'Princess of Parrallelograms'.

Although such education was unusual for females at the time, Ada's mother believed it would supress her imagination. Lady Byron believed poetry was the source of Lord Byron's 'ill disposition', prompted by his imagination. She believed subjects such as mathematics and music to be the counter to this poetic, imaginitive nature and hoped that by studying them, Ada would not inherit her father's disposition. Wikipedia has more information on Ada.

Ada, the Programmer

While not widley acknowledged during her life, Ada's legacy was her work with Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine. Although a full model was never built at the time, the analytical engine was designed to perform complex calculations. In modern times, a working model has been created to Babbage's exact specifications.

Mathematically and scientifically talented, Ada recognised potential in Babbage's analytical engine to go beyond pure calculation. She identified the potential for the machine to become a general-purpose computer while translating Italian mathemetician Louis Menebrea's memoir on the engine. In addition to translating, Ada included a set of her own notes on the subject. In her notes she details the first computer algorithm and details how other information, such as letters, can be encoded for the machine to work with. She even speculated as to the introduction of applications such as computer-generated music.

Ada's Algorithm

Known as Note G, for the translation note she recorded it in, the algorithm Ada wrote for the difference engine was intended to calculate Bernoulli numbers. Given a parameter n, her algorithm would calculate the numerator and denomenator for the nth Bernoulli number.
